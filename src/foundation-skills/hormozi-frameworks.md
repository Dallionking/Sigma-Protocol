# Hormozi Frameworks Skill

This skill provides Alex Hormozi's proven frameworks from $100M Offers, $100M Leads, and Gym Launch Secrets.

## Framework 1: The Value Equation

Every feature, offer, and pricing decision must maximize perceived value:

```
Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood) / (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)
```

### Application Checklist

- [ ] **Dream Outcome**: What is the ideal end-state the customer desires?
- [ ] **Perceived Likelihood**: How confident are they that your solution will work?
- [ ] **Time Delay**: How quickly will they see results?
- [ ] **Effort & Sacrifice**: How much work/pain is required from them?

### Scoring Guide

| Factor | Poor (1) | Average (5) | Excellent (10) |
|--------|----------|-------------|----------------|
| Dream Outcome | Generic benefit | Specific improvement | Life-changing transformation |
| Perceived Likelihood | No proof | Some testimonials | Guaranteed results |
| Time Delay | Months/years | Weeks | Days/immediate |
| Effort & Sacrifice | Significant work | Moderate effort | Effortless |

## Framework 2: Grand Slam Offer Construction

A "Grand Slam Offer" is so good that people feel stupid saying no.

### Components

1. **The Dream Outcome** (What they really want)
2. **The Perceived Likelihood of Achievement** (Why they'll get it)
3. **Time to Achievement** (How fast they'll see results)
4. **Effort & Sacrifice** (How easy you make it)

### The Offer Stack

Build value by stacking bonuses that solve objections:

```markdown
## Core Offer
- [Main Product/Service] - Value: $X

## Bonus 1: Speed Bonus
- [Helps them get results faster] - Value: $X

## Bonus 2: Effort Reducer
- [Makes it easier for them] - Value: $X

## Bonus 3: Risk Reversal
- [Guarantee that removes their fear] - Value: $X

## Total Value: $X,XXX
## Your Price: $XXX (X% discount)
```

## Framework 5: Risk Reversal Strategies

Remove all risk from the customer:

### Types of Guarantees

| Type | Example | Risk Level |
|------|---------|------------|
| **Unconditional** | "30-day money back, no questions" | Highest (you) |
| **Conditional** | "If you do X and don't get Y, refund" | Medium |
| **Anti-Guarantee** | "All sales final - this isn't for everyone" | Lowest (them) |
| **Outcome-Based** | "We'll work for free until you hit $X" | Performance |

### The "Better Than Money Back" Guarantee

Don't just offer refunds. Offer to pay them if it doesn't work:

> "If you follow the program and don't get [RESULT] in [TIMEFRAME], I'll refund 100% of your money AND give you $500 for wasting your time."

## Framework 6: Pricing Psychology

### The Pricing Ladder

1. **Free** - Lead magnet (email, guide)
2. **Low-ticket** ($7-$97) - Self-solve, low touch
3. **Mid-ticket** ($500-$2,000) - Some support, group
4. **High-ticket** ($3,000-$30,000) - Done-with-you
5. **Premium** ($30,000+) - Done-for-you

### The Value Staircase

Price is justified by the transformation gap:

```
Current State ──────────────────────► Desired State
              [Your Solution]
              
Price = (Value of Desired State) - (Value of Current State)
```
