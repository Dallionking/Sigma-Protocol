## Quick Framework Reference

### NESB Triggers (Kyle Milligan)

Every piece of copy needs 3+ of these:

| Trigger | Ask Yourself |
|---------|-------------|
| **N**ew | Does this feel like a fresh discovery? |
| **E**asy | Does this feel achievable without heroic effort? |
| **S**afe | Have you removed all perceived risk? |
| **B**ig | Is the promised transformation significant? |

### Five Drivers (Blair Warren)

> "People will do anything for those who encourage their dreams, justify their failures, allay their fears, confirm their suspicions, and help them throw rocks at their enemies."

| Driver | Copy Application |
|--------|-----------------|
| **Encourage Dreams** | Hero section, CTA |
| **Justify Failures** | Problem section ("not your fault") |
| **Allay Fears** | Guarantee, FAQ |
| **Confirm Suspicions** | Agitation ("you were right") |
| **Throw Rocks** | Enemy identification |

### Market Awareness (Schwartz)

| Level | Strategy |
|-------|----------|
| Unaware | Story-first |
| Problem Aware | Agitate, introduce solution |
| Solution Aware | Differentiate mechanism |
| Product Aware | Objection handling |
| Most Aware | Best offer, simple CTA |

### Cialdini's 7 Principles

1. **Reciprocity** - Give value first
2. **Commitment** - Small yes → big yes
3. **Social Proof** - Others are doing it
4. **Authority** - Expert credibility
5. **Liking** - Story, similarity
6. **Scarcity** - Limited availability
7. **Unity** - Shared identity/tribe
